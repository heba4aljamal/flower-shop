Sunflowers

Sunflowers are annual plants that belong to the genus Helianthus. They are characterized by their large, yellow ray flowers which resemble the sun. Sunflowers have a tall, sturdy stem and typically grow in warm climates. They are known for their ability to track the sun throughout the day, a phenomenon called heliotropism. Sunflowers are cultivated for their seeds, which are a nutritious snack, and for their oil, which is used in cooking and cosmetics. Additionally, they are popular ornamental plants in gardens and landscapes.
